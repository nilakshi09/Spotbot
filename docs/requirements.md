## 1. Overview

Spotbot is a B2B SaaS platform that provides **influencer audience fraud detection** for marketing and talent agencies. Users enter an Instagram or YouTube handle and receive a fraud score, estimated real reach, and a shareable report — instantly, with zero onboarding friction.

### 1.1 Goals

- Enable agencies to verify influencer audience authenticity before signing contracts
- Deliver fraud analysis in **seconds**, not days
- Produce client-ready reports that drop into existing approval workflows
- Operate as a fully self-serve product with no sales-call requirement

### 1.2 Success Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Scan completion time | < 30 seconds per handle |
| Fraud score accuracy | ≥ 85% agreement with manual expert audit |
| User activation | 60%+ of signups complete their first scan |
| Report share rate | 30%+ of generated reports are shared or exported |
| Monthly churn | < 5% for paid seats |

## 2. User Personas

### 2.1 Agency Analyst

- **Role**: Evaluates influencer candidates for brand campaigns
- **Goal**: Quickly verify audience authenticity across a shortlist of 20–50 creators
- **Pain**: Currently relies on manual spot-checks and gut instinct; needs data-backed confidence
- **Usage**: Runs 50–200 scans/month, exports reports for client decks

### 2.2 Agency Account Manager

- **Role**: Manages client relationships and campaign approvals
- **Goal**: Present influencer recommendations with verifiable audience quality data
- **Pain**: Clients increasingly demand proof of audience authenticity; existing tools are too slow
- **Usage**: Reviews reports generated by analysts, shares with clients, 10–30 reports/month

### 2.3 Freelance Marketer

- **Role**: Independent consultant running influencer campaigns for small brands
- **Goal**: Protect reputation by avoiding influencers with fake audiences
- **Pain**: Cannot afford enterprise platforms; needs a low-cost, self-serve solution
- **Usage**: Runs 5–20 scans/month on a starter plan

## 7. Fraud Model Specification

### 7.1 Signal Definitions

#### Follower Growth Velocity (Weight: 25%)

- **Input**: Follower count snapshots over time (daily granularity preferred)
- **Method**: Calculate day-over-day growth rate; flag days where growth exceeds 3σ above the account's rolling 30-day average
- **Output**: Score 0–100 (higher = more suspicious)
- **Threshold**: ≥ 3 spikes in 90 days without correlating viral content → high risk

#### Engagement Rate Benchmarking (Weight: 30%)

- **Input**: Average likes + comments per post (last 20 posts), follower count, account niche/category
- **Method**: Compare engagement rate (interactions / followers × 100) against benchmark distribution for same niche and follower tier
- **Output**: Score 0–100 based on percentile deviation from benchmark median
- **Threshold**: Engagement rate < 40% of niche median → high risk

#### Comment Sentiment Analysis (Weight: 25%)

- **Input**: Text of last 200 comments across recent posts
- **Method**: NLP classification of each comment into categories: authentic, generic/bot, emoji-only, spam
- **Output**: Score 0–100 based on ratio of non-authentic comments
- **Threshold**: > 35% bot-pattern comments → high risk

#### Spike Detection (Weight: 20%)

- **Input**: Follower timeline + posting history
- **Method**: Cross-reference follower spikes against content posting events; flag spikes with no corresponding post, viral event, or media mention
- **Output**: Score 0–100 based on number and magnitude of unexplained spikes
- **Threshold**: ≥ 2 unexplained spikes of > 5,000 followers → high risk

### 7.2 Composite Score Calculation

```
fraud_score = (growth_velocity × 0.25)
            + (engagement_rate × 0.30)
            + (comment_sentiment × 0.25)
            + (spike_detection × 0.20)
```

### 7.3 Risk Classification

| Score Range | Risk Level | Label | Color |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0–29 | Low | ✅ Clean | Green |
| 30–59 | Medium | ⚠️ Review | Amber |
| 60–100 | High | 🚨 Suspicious | Red |

- CRM integrations (HubSpot, Salesforce)
